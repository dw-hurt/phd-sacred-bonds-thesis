## Document Overview

This 9-page theoretical paper published in *NeuroQuantology* presents an innovative interdisciplinary bridge connecting Carl Jung's concept of synchronicity with modern quantum physics, specifically quantum entanglement, quantum non-locality, quantum coherence, and quantum superposition. Limar proposes that biological molecules—particularly DNA involved in cell division (mitosis and meiosis)—may serve as material carriers of consciousness, providing a physical substrate for synchronistic phenomena. The paper positions quantum non-locality as "one of the most adequate physical mechanisms" for explaining Jung's hypothesis of meaningful acausal coincidences.

**Keywords:** consciousness, synchronicity, quantum entanglement, nucleic acids, mitosis, meiosis

---

## Main Thesis

Limar's central argument is threefold:

1. **Quantum Non-Locality as Physical Mechanism**: Quantum non-locality, particularly as demonstrated in the solution to the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen (EPR) paradox, offers a physically plausible mechanism for synchronicity. Entangled quantum particles remain instantaneously correlated across distance without causal connection—a phenomenon analogous to Jung's acausal connecting principle.

2. **DNA as Consciousness Carrier**: Biological molecules involved in cell division (mitosis and meiosis), especially DNA, may function as material carriers of consciousness. This proposal is grounded in Jung's phenomenology of analytical psychology, which treats consciousness as fundamental at the interface of psyche and matter.

3. **Molecular Biology Bridge**: By connecting quantum physics, molecular biology, and analytical psychology, Limar attempts a "psychophysiological substantiation of synchronicity," suggesting that meaningful coincidences may be undergirded by real (albeit non-classical) physical processes occurring in genetic material.

## Key Theoretical Concepts

### 1. Quantum Non-Locality
- **Definition**: Phenomenon where particles once entangled remain connected such that actions on one affect the other, regardless of distance
- **Relevance**: Provides a mechanism for acausal connections between spatially separated events
- **EPR Paradox**: Quantum non-locality resolves Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen's challenge to quantum mechanics by demonstrating instantaneous correlations without hidden variables
- **Synchronicity Parallel**: Like entangled particles, synchronistic events exhibit meaningful correlation without causal linkage

### 2. Quantum Coherence
- **Definition**: Maintenance of ordered, phase-correlated quantum states within biological molecules
- **Biological Context**: Believed to underlie quantum computation in living cells
- **Consciousness Connection**: Coherent quantum states persist long enough to participate in consciousness-related processes
- **Synchronicity Application**: Provides mechanism for sustained entangled states relevant to consciousness and meaningful coincidence

### 3. Quantum Superposition
- **Definition**: Quantum entities exist in multiple states simultaneously until measured (Schrödinger's Cat)
- **Title Reference**: "Schrödinger's Cat 'Wanders' Between Chromosomes" invokes this principle
- **Consciousness Implication**: Multiple potential outcomes exist simultaneously in quantum substrates of consciousness
- **Synchronicity Connection**: Superposition allows multiple possibilities to manifest as meaningful coincidences in macro-reality

### 4. DNA and Cell Division
- **Mitosis & Meiosis**: Cell division processes proposed as sites of quantum coherence and consciousness
- **Biological Substrate**: DNA molecules provide physical anchor for entanglement-like properties of consciousness
- **Material Carrier**: Bridges Jung's "psychoid" realm (neither purely psychic nor purely material) with tangible biological structures
- **Molecular Route**: Offers pathway for non-local synchronistic phenomena to manifest in living systems
