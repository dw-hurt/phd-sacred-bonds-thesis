## Source: Fayyaz (2019)

### Quote 1: Universal Human Bonding Need
**Citation:** Fayyaz, N. M. (2019, p. 75)

> "Despite their countless differences, human beings in these societies are bound by their innate need to form relationships and engage in mating behavior. To successfully fulfil this need, human beings tend to adopt different strategies, either long-term or short-term."

**Use for Chapter 1:** Establishes universal human mating imperative across cultures

**Integration Notes:** Use in Section 1.1 to introduce mating as fundamental human behavior

---

### Quote 2: Civilizational Shift to Monogamy ⭐ CRITICAL
**Citation:** Fayyaz, N. M. (2019, p. 79)

> "In order to ensure that Homo Sapiens (i.e. the modern human beings) evolved into a civilized society, the mating behavior had to shift towards monogamy instead of polygamy."

**Use for Chapter 1:** KEY ARGUMENT - Modern crisis as reversal of civilizational evolution toward monogamy

**Integration Notes:** Use in Section 1.2 to establish evolutionary context for contemporary crisis. This quote supports the argument that modern dating environment represents a "de-civilization" or reversal of evolved monogamous adaptations.

---

### Quote 3: Ancestral Problems Persist
**Citation:** Fayyaz, N. M. (2019, p. 75-76)

> "Our ancestral sexual behavior has evolved into a more current sophisticated system of mate selection, yet holds the same set of adaptive problems."

**Use for Chapter 1:** Mismatch theory - sophisticated modern system confronting ancient problems

**Integration Notes:** Use in Section 1.3 to introduce mismatch framework - ancestral adaptive problems persist despite modern technological sophistication

---

### Quote 4: Hasty Generalization Problem
**Citation:** Fayyaz, N. M. (2019, p. 76)

> "Different adaptive problems require different adaptive solutions. We cannot have a 'general solution' as there are no 'general problems'."

**Use for Chapter 1:** Critique of one-size-fits-all evolutionary explanations, supports six-dimensional model

**Integration Notes:** Use in Section 1.4 to justify need for multi-dimensional framework rather than reductionist approach

---

### Quote 5: Need for Cultural Perspective
**Citation:** Fayyaz, N. M. (2019, p. 79)

> "As human beings are social beings, it would be necessary to consider how social interactions in different cultures influence the norms of human sexual behavior."

**Use for Chapter 1:** Justifies sociological dimension in dissertation framework

**Integration Notes:** Use in Section 1.5 when introducing the six-dimensional model, specifically to justify Dimension 2 (Sociological/Environmental)
